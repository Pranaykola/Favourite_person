My Favourite People

Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, whose full name was Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, stands as one of India’s most revered scientists and leaders. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, into a humble family, he overcame early financial hardships through determination and hard work. Kalam’s academic journey began at St. Joseph’s College, Tiruchirappalli, where he studied Physics, followed by Aeronautical Engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology. Kalam’s career began at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) in 1958, and he later joined the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). At ISRO, he played a pivotal role in developing India’s first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-3), which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in 1980, marking a major milestone in India’s space program. His leadership in missile technology, particularly with the Agni and Prithvi missiles, earned him the title “Missile Man of India”. He was instrumental in advancing India’s nuclear capabilities, notably during the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi, often called the 'Father of the Nation', was a leader who fought for India's freedom from British rule. He believed in non-violence. Every year on October 2nd, Mahatma Gandhi's birthday is celebrated as Gandhi Jayanti to honour his efforts in freeing India.His idea of 'Ahimsa,' which means not hurting anyone, was widely admired and followed by many influential people worldwide. He became an indomitable figure who couldn't be defeated in any situation. Mahatma Gandhi initiated the 'Khadi Movement' to encourage the use of fabrics like khadi or jute..Gandhi strongly supported agriculture and encouraged people to engage in farming. He inspired Indians to embrace manual labour and emphasized self-reliance, urging them to provide for their needs and lead simple lives. He began weaving cotton clothes using the Charkha to reduce dependence on foreign goods and promote Swadeshi products among Indians. During the fight for India's freedom, Gandhiji faced imprisonment several times along with his followers, but his main goal was always the freedom of his motherland. Even when he was in prison, he never chose the path of violence.

Bhagat Singh

Bhagat Singh was a prominent Indian socialist revolutionary whose acts of defiance against the British Raj made him a hero of the Indian independence movement. Born in 1907, he was deeply affected by events like the Jallianwala Bagh massacre and the death of Lala Lajpat Rai, which fueled his desire for revolutionary action. He joined the Hindustan Socialist Republican Association (HSRA) and participated in acts of protest, including the bombing of the Central Legislative Assembly and the assassination of British police officer John Saunders. Bhagat Singh was arrested, tried, and ultimately executed at the young age of 23, along with his associates Sukhdev and Rajguru, on March 23, 1931. His sacrifice and revolutionary ideals continue to inspire generations in India. Bhagat Singh was a courageous Indian freedom fighter who played a key role in India’s struggle for independence.
